# FFT Story Walkthrough - AI Quick Reference

## Level Recommendations

| Chapter | Story Level Range | Notes |
|---------|------------------|-------|
| Ch.1 | 1-9 | Enemies scale with story; no grinding needed |
| Ch.2 | 10-23 | Dorter is a spike at Lv.10; grind if struggling |
| Ch.3 | 25-36 | Riovanes at end is brutal; prepare Ramza specifically |
| Ch.4 | 41-60+ | Long chapter; side quests available mid-chapter |

Random encounters scale to your party's average level. Story battles have fixed levels.

## Difficulty Spike Guide

### Dorter Trade City (Ch.1 #5 / Ch.2 #11)
- Elevated Archer has height advantage and picks off your units
- Black Mages hit hard with AoE; rain boosts Thunder in Ch.2 version
- **Fix:** Get your own Black Mage on high ground. Focus fire mages before melee.

### Wiegraf 1v1 at Riovanes (#31) -- THE WALL
- **Phase 1:** Solo Ramza vs Wiegraf. No allies. If you saved inside Riovanes, you may be trapped.
- **Phase 2:** Wiegraf transforms into Belias with Archaeodaemons. Full party joins.
- **SAVE OUTSIDE RIOVANES in a separate slot!**
- **Best Ramza build:** Monk with Tailwind (from Mettle). Spam Tailwind to outspeed Wiegraf. Use Chakra to self-heal. Shockwave avoids Counter.
- **Alternative:** Monk with Dual Wield from Ninja. Raw damage race.
- Phase 2: Agrias Holy Sword, Mustadio Leg Shot on demons, -ja spells from Black Mage.

### Riovanes Roof (#32)
- Rapha is an AI guest who rushes into melee and dies to Assassin instant-kill (Suffocate)
- **Fix:** DPS race. Ignore Elmdore. Burst one Assassin to critical HP = battle ends.
- Strip your own gear so Assassins target your units instead of Rapha (counterintuitive but works).

## Missable Content and Points of No Return

| Warning | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Riovanes save trap** | If you save inside Riovanes with only one slot, you may be stuck at Wiegraf forever. Always keep a backup save. |
| **Orbonne final dungeon** | After entering Monastery Vaults 4th Floor (battle 49), you CANNOT return to the world map. Save in a separate slot before entering. |
| **Aerith's flower** | In Sal Ghidos (Ch.4), a flower girl offers a flower. YOU MUST BUY IT. Required for Cloud and other sidequests. |
| **Keep Mustadio/Beowulf/Reis alive** | If any of these die permanently (crystal/treasure), you lose access to the Beowulf/Reis questline and Cloud recruitment. |
| **Keep Agrias alive** | Required for a late-game sidequest after Mullonde. |

## Side Quests (All Chapter 4)

### Beowulf & Reis (Available after Zeltennia)
1. Visit Goug for a cutscene (need Mustadio in party)
2. Go to Mining Town of Gollund and read the Haunted Mine rumor
3. Go to Lesalia -- Beowulf appears and asks to join; accept him as guest
4. Travel to Gollund; clear 4 Colliery battles (Lv.40+)
5. Defeat Synoguhe (Archaeodaemon boss) to free Reis (Holy Dragon form)
6. Return to Goug -- Reis transforms to human form

### Cloud Recruitment (After Fort Besselat)
1. Must have Mustadio; must have bought flower from Sal Ghidos
2. Visit Goug -- Mustadio activates a machine; go to Zeltennia bar for Nelveska rumor
3. Complete Nelveska Temple battle (also yields Javelin II, Escutcheon II via Treasure Hunter)
4. Return to Goug -- Cloud is summoned, then runs off to Sal Ghidos
5. Go to Sal Ghidos -- fight a battle protecting Cloud; he joins afterwards
6. Cloud starts at Lv.1 with no equipment. Needs heavy leveling.
7. Find Materia Blade at Mount Bervenia (Treasure Hunter on highest peak, Jump 3+) for his Limit skills.

### Midlight's Deep / Deep Dungeon (After clearing Mullonde Cathedral Sanctuary)
1. Go to Port City of Warjilis -- a bar cutscene triggers and dungeon entrance appears on world map
2. 10 floors of increasingly difficult battles (Lv.60+)
3. Floors are dark; must step on hidden tiles to illuminate
4. Contains the strongest equipment in the game (Excalibur, Chaos Blade, etc.)
5. Final floor boss: Elidibus

### Nelveska Temple (Part of Cloud recruitment chain)
- Fight a battle against a Construct, Hydras, and Cockatrices
- Use Treasure Hunter to get Javelin II and Escutcheon II from the tops of the two pillars (need Jump 4+ and a large monster as a stepping stone)
